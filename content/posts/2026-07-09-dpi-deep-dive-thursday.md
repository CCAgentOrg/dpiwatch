# DPI Deep Dive — Thursday | July 09, 2026

**Layer L4: Commerce & Logistics — ONDC, GeM, and India's Digital Commerce Stack**

This week's Commerce & Logistics layer saw one of the most significant geopolitical validation moments for India's DPI model: Indonesia officially launching its own ONDC-inspired open commerce network during PM Modi's state visit to Jakarta. Back home, ONDC continued its exponential growth trajectory, crossing major milestones in buyer adoption and network reach, while GeM participated in the Vibrant Gujarat Regional Conferences and crossed ₹18.4 lakh crore in cumulative procurement value.

---

## 1. Indonesia Launches ION — ONDC Goes Global

The headline story of the week is the formal launch of the **Indonesia Open Network (ION)**, an interoperable digital commerce platform built directly on the architecture of India's ONDC. The announcement came during PM Modi's state visit to Indonesia on July 6–8, 2026, where both nations adopted a Joint Statement deepening their Comprehensive Strategic Partnership across defence, maritime security, trade, technology, and digital connectivity.

ION targets Indonesia's 65 million+ micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs), aiming to create a more open and inclusive digital marketplace. According to Indonesia's reporting, the platform is already in the technical stage with MSME onboarding underway. The cooperation goes beyond an MoU — Indonesian teams have travelled to India to study the DPI policy framework, and the architecture has been adapted to Indonesia's regulatory and market context.

This is a watershed moment for India's DPI export thesis. ONDC was conceived as a domestically-focused open network to counter platform monopolies, but its adoption by the world's fourth-largest nation (by population) validates the architectural approach at a global scale. The cross-layer connections here are significant: ION builds on the same open protocol thinking that drove UPI's international expansion, and it positions India as a blueprint-provider for digital commerce governance in the Global South.

**Sources:** PM of India official outcomes list[^1]; DD India[^2]; Daily Pioneer[^3]; VOI Indonesia[^4]

## 2. ONDC's Exponential Growth: 20 Crore Buyers, 5 Lakh Sellers, 1,000 Cities

A PIB backgrounder released to mark 11 years of Digital India (July 1, 2026) confirmed ONDC's latest network-scale numbers. By June 2026:

- **20 crore+ buyers** on the network
- **5 lakh+ sellers** registered
- **Presence in 1,000 cities** across India
- **Nearly 90 lakh monthly transactions**
- **India Post integrated** as a logistics service provider

Separately, research reports this week noted that ONDC has crossed **1.16 lakh sellers across 630 cities**, with its D2C market reaching **₹108 billion** — growing two to three times faster than the traditional e-commerce market. The network's commission structure (around 3% versus 15–25% on incumbent platforms) continues to be the key value proposition for sellers.

These numbers represent dramatic acceleration. ONDC launched in 2022 with a handful of pilot transactions; reaching 90 lakh monthly orders within four years is a pace that rivals UPI's early growth curve. The India Post logistics integration is particularly notable — it extends reliable delivery infrastructure to Tier-3 and Tier-4 cities where private logistics networks remain thin, directly addressing the last-mile problem that has constrained e-commerce penetration in rural India.

**Sources:** PIB Digital India Backgrounder[^5]; Instagram/viral data points from Zane Marketing[^6]

## 3. ONDC Financial Services: Credit Disbursals Accelerate in June

ONDC's ambitions extend well beyond retail commerce. A June 2026 performance update shared by ONDC's financial services team highlighted growing traction in embedded lending on the network:

- **Loan disbursals grew** through ONDC's credit rails, with the network facilitating buy-now-pay-later and seller-working-capital products
- The vision for **open and interoperable financial services** on the commerce network — where a buyer on any app can access credit from any lender — is starting to materialise

While specific disbursal volumes were not disclosed, the direction is clear: ONDC is positioning itself as a full-stack commerce AND financial services protocol, not merely a product discovery layer. This cross-layer play connects L4 (Commerce) directly with L2 (Payments) — ONDC transactions settle via UPI, and credit products on the network are underwritten using digital identity data from L1 (Aadhaar/eKYC).

This embedded finance model, if executed at scale, could dramatically reduce the cost of credit for small sellers by eliminating the platform intermediary that currently captures lending margins on closed ecosystems like Amazon Pay and Flipkart Pay Later.

**Source:** LinkedIn/ONDC Financial Services update[^7]

## 4. eSARAS × ONDC: Rural Women's SHGs Get National Market Access

A current affairs brief on July 2, 2026 highlighted the growing integration between **eSARAS (SARAS Aajeevika)** — the digital marketplace for women-led Self-Help Groups under DAY-NRLM — and the ONDC network. Key milestones:

- **800+ handcrafted products** from SHGs listed on ONDC
- Products visible across **11+ buyer apps** on the network
- Access to ONDC's **20 crore+ buyer base**
- eSARAS also available on **UMANG** (796+ crore transactions logged as of June 2026)

The eSARAS-to-ONDC pipeline is a textbook example of how digital commerce DPI can be layered on top of livelihood missions. Products range from Chanderi sarees (Madhya Pradesh) to Pashmina (Jammu & Kashmir) to marble crafts (Rajasthan). By onboarding onto ONDC, these artisanal products compete for visibility alongside mainstream retail — something that would have been impossible on a single-silo marketplace.

The brief also noted the launch of the **SARAS Shakti Collection**, a premium curated gift range targeting institutional and corporate markets. Future convergence with GeM for government gifting procurement could create a powerful demand loop.

**Source:** NEXT IAS Current Affairs[^8]

## 5. GeM Crosses ₹18.4 Lakh Crore GMV; Pushes MSME Onboarding at Vibrant Gujarat

The PIB backgrounder confirmed that **Government e-Marketplace (GeM)** has recorded cumulative GMV of **over ₹18.4 lakh crore** as of June 2026, including **₹5 lakh crore in FY 2025–26 alone**. The platform has enabled over **11 lakh MSMEs** to access government procurement markets.

GeM participated in the **Vibrant Gujarat Regional Conferences** held from June 29 to July 3, 2026 at GSFC University, Vadodara. The conference brought together government departments, PSUs, and financial institutions to drive procurement awareness and seller onboarding. GeM's CEO, Mihir Kumar, also addressed the CII MSME RISE Summit 2026, underscoring market access as the foundation of MSME growth.

On the product side, GeM introduced a new feature: the **negotiation option for item-wise service bids converted from Bid to Reverse Auction**, giving both buyers and sellers more flexibility in procurement processes. Meanwhile, tightened **BIS/IS certification requirements** for product categories signal GeM's maturation from a quantity-driven marketplace to one focused on quality and compliance.

**Sources:** PIB[^5]; GeM Facebook[^9]; GeM Tender Service[^10]

## Cross-Layer Connections

This week's developments highlight how Layer L4 (Commerce & Logistics) is increasingly intertwined with other DPI layers:

| Connection | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| **L4 → L2 (Payments)** | ONDC transactions settle via UPI; embedded credit products on the network rely on UPI rails |
| **L4 → L1 (Identity)** | Seller onboarding on ONDC/GeM uses Aadhaar-based eKYC for trust and verification |
| **L4 → L3 (Data Exchange)** | eSARAS integration with ONDC demonstrates how document exchange and market access layers converge |
| **L4 → L6 (Governance)** | GeM's government procurement digitisation feeds into broader e-governance objectives |
| **L4 → Global** | ION's launch is the first full international adoption of an Indian DPI protocol for commerce |

The Indonesia ION launch is the most strategically significant development. If Indonesia — a G20 member with 270 million people — can successfully operationalise an ONDC-style network, it opens the door for similar adoptions across ASEAN, Africa, and Latin America. India is no longer just building DPI for itself; it is becoming the architect of global digital commerce infrastructure.
