# DPI Deep Dive — Thursday | July 10, 2026

India's Commerce & Logistics DPI layer — built around ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce) and GeM (Government e-Marketplace) — had a remarkable week. From the first international deployment of ONDC's architecture in Indonesia, to AI-powered procurement automation on GeM, to grassroots ONDC onboarding in Jammu & Kashmir's districts, the layer is simultaneously scaling globally, modernizing domestically, and penetrating deep into India's hinterlands. Here are the five most consequential developments from July 3–10, 2026.

## 1. Indonesia's ION Marks the First Live Transaction on an ONDC-Inspired Network

The most geopolitically significant development this week was the formal handshake between India's ONDC and Indonesia's **Indonesia Open Network (ION)**. During Prime Minister Narendra Modi's State Visit to Jakarta (July 6–8), the two countries witnessed what is expected to be ION's **first live transaction** at the Modi-Prabowo Subianto summit on July 7.

ION is explicitly modeled on ONDC's open-network architecture. Indonesia — the world's fourth most populous nation with 280 million people — is adapting India's interoperable digital commerce playbook to serve its **65 million+ micro, small, and medium enterprises**. Unlike UPI's international expansions (France, Singapore, UAE, Sri Lanka), which primarily enable cross-border payments for Indian travellers, Indonesia's adoption is structurally different: Jakarta wants to **replicate the entire DPI stack**, not just one layer.

The joint statement issued on July 7 confirmed MoUs on cooperation in telecommunications technologies and services, and deeper financial integration. But the ONDC-ION connection is the standout — it represents the first large-scale international deployment of India's digital commerce architecture in a major emerging economy.

**Why it matters:** India has long positioned its DPI model as "exportable infrastructure." UPI went first. Now ONDC follows. If ION succeeds, it validates the thesis that open, interoperable digital commerce networks can work at scale beyond India's borders — and creates a template for similar adoptions across ASEAN. The L4 layer, once purely domestic, is becoming a tool of digital diplomacy.

**Cross-layer connections:** This development sits at the intersection of L2 (UPI-QRIS integration was also discussed during the visit), L1 (digital identity infrastructure for Indonesian MSMEs), and L3 (data exchange protocols between the two networks). India's DPI stack doesn't export in isolation — it exports as an integrated system.

## 2. GeM Crosses ₹18.4 Lakh Crore in Cumulative Procurement; Launches AI-Powered Bid Evaluation

On the GeM front, the platform continued its relentless expansion. According to data highlighted during World MSME Day (June 27) and reiterated in subsequent reporting this week, GeM clocked approximately **₹43 lakh crore in order value in April 2026 alone**, pushing cumulative procurement past **₹18.4 lakh crore**. India's government procurement market crossed **₹5.4 lakh crore in FY25 on GeM alone**.

Three significant platform developments were reported this week:

**GeM Enhanced Network Intelligence Engine (GENIE):** On June 13, ECL (Eastern Coalfields) and NIT Durgapur launched GENIE — an in-house developed **AI-powered procurement automation application**. The tool is designed to automate bid processing, evaluation, and output generation for submitted bid documents. Multiple coal sector PSUs (CMPDI Ranchi, SAIL Rourkela) are now adopting GENIE, signalling that AI-driven procurement is moving from pilot to production across the public sector.

**AI-Based Tender Evaluation System:** A broader Expression of Interest (EOI) was floated for procuring an "AI Based Tender Evaluation System for end-to-end automated processing, evaluation, and output generation of submitted bid documents" — indicating GeM's central platform itself is preparing to embed AI deeper into its core bidding workflows.

**New e-Waste V2 Categories:** GeM is expanding its catalogue with new e-Waste V2 categories, tightening compliance for old pending orders, and introducing AI-powered bid evaluation at the platform level.

**Why it matters:** GeM has evolved from a simple B2B government procurement portal into an increasingly intelligent procurement operating system. The introduction of GENIE and the broader AI tender evaluation EOI signal that India's public procurement is entering a phase where algorithmic decision-making handles a growing share of the evaluation workload — reducing human discretion, potentially speeding up procurement cycles, but also raising important questions about algorithmic transparency and vendor fairness.

## 3. ONDC Clocks 218 Million Transactions; D2C Commerce Emerges as the Growth Vector

A widely cited statistic from this week's MSME Day reporting: ONDC has now clocked approximately **218 million cumulative transactions** on the network. While the network started with food delivery and grocery, the most significant structural shift is the emergence of **D2C (Direct-to-Consumer) brands** as a major growth vector.

Content creators and business analysts this week highlighted ONDC's D2C proposition: a **~3% commission rate** compared to the 15-30% charged by traditional marketplaces like Amazon and Flipkart, serving a market estimated at **₹108 billion**. New buyer platforms like Zillybuy are gaining traction as web-first experiences that require no app installation — lowering the barrier for both sellers and buyers.

ONDC COO **Rohit Lohia** was named to the Grand Jury Panel of the e4m D2C Awards 5.0 this week, underscoring how central ONDC has become to India's D2C ecosystem. Lohia, who previously held senior roles at Paytm and co-founded fintech/e-commerce startups, has been focused on "accelerating the growth of India's open digital commerce ecosystem by enabling transparent access, innovation, and sustainable value creation."

Meanwhile, Walmart-owned Flipkart is reportedly **evaluating whether to build a standalone platform or leverage the ONDC network** for its own operations — a remarkable signal from the incumbent that the open network is becoming too large to ignore.

**Why it matters:** The D2C pivot is the most important strategic development on ONDC this quarter. While food delivery brought volume and visibility, D2C brands represent the network's sustainable economic model: brands that were previously locked into high-commission marketplace ecosystems now have a credible alternative. If Flipkart eventually joins ONDC in any capacity, it would be the network's most significant network-effect catalyst.

## 4. GeM-ONDC Integration and National Cooperation Policy 2025: Cooperatives Enter Digital Commerce

The **National Cooperation Policy 2025**, launched by Union Minister Amit Shah earlier this month, formally integrates both GeM and ONDC as the two designated digital commerce platforms for India's cooperative sector. This is a structural policy development with long-term implications.

Under the policy, cooperatives are expected to:
- Register on **GeM** to access government procurement opportunities
- Integrate with **ONDC** to sell products and services to consumers through the open network

Multiple state-level implementation workshops were reported this week, including in **Sikkim** where officials from leading organizations including ITPO Bharat Mandapam participated in roadmapping sessions for effective implementation of the National Cooperation Policy, emphasizing the coordinated action needed for cooperative digital transformation.

The Ministry of MSME also conducted workshops covering GeM registration, public procurement, e-commerce through ONDC, export marketing, and NSIC schemes — a combined DPI-onboarding approach that treats GeM and ONDC as complementary platforms rather than competitors.

**Why it matters:** India's cooperative sector — spanning dairy (Amul), banking, agriculture, housing, and consumer goods — represents an enormous but digitally underserved segment. The National Cooperation Policy's explicit GeM+ONDC integration mandate means these platforms will see a wave of cooperative registrations in the coming months, potentially adding millions of new sellers and buyers to both ecosystems.

## 5. Mission YUVA Brings ONDC to Jammu & Kashmir's Districts; Grassroots Digital Commerce Expands

Perhaps the most heartening — and least reported — development was the grassroots ONDC onboarding happening in Jammu & Kashmir. The **Mission YUVA** platform, a J&K government initiative for youth employment and entrepreneurship, is being used as the vehicle to onboard local shops and commercial establishments onto ONDC.

In **Kishtwar district**, Assistant Labour Commissioner Mamta Sudershan (JKAS) initiated the onboarding of shops and establishments, with officials reporting that **63 shops and commercial establishments** have already been successfully onboarded for ONDC integration. Similar drives were launched in **Shopian**, **Bandipora**, and **Pulwama** districts, with officials issuing circulars directing registered establishments to onboard within 30 days.

The district-level approach is significant: rather than waiting for sellers to discover ONDC organically, government officials are proactively bringing the network to local businesses — kirana stores, restaurants, retailers, and service providers — and using Mission YUVA as the onboarding application.

**Why it matters:** ONDC's long-term success depends not on metropolitan D2C brands (they will find the network anyway) but on whether India's ~13 crore small retailers — the backbone of Indian commerce — can be brought onto a digital commerce network at scale. Mission YUVA's district-level drives represent the most organized attempt yet to crack this challenge. If the J&K model can be replicated nationally, it could unlock the network's true potential.

## Layer Assessment: L4 Commerce & Logistics at an Inflection Point

This week crystallized three concurrent trends for India's Commerce & Logistics DPI:

1. **Global export of the architecture** — ONDC-inspired ION in Indonesia marks the beginning of international adoption, with UPI integration creating a cross-layer digital bridge.
2. **AI transformation of public procurement** — GeM's GENIE deployment and AI tender evaluation EOI signal that India's ₹5+ lakh crore government procurement market is entering an algorithmic era.
3. **Grassroots penetration deepens** — Mission YUVA's district-level ONDC onboarding and the cooperative sector's mandated GeM+ONDC integration are expanding the network's addressable market to include India's most underserved commercial segments.

The L4 layer is no longer just about competing with Amazon and Flipkart. It's becoming a pillar of digital diplomacy, a platform for AI-driven governance, and a vehicle for economic inclusion — simultaneously.
