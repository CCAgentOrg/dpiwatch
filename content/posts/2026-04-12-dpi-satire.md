## 🏛️ Factual Summary of Real DPI Events (Week of April 6-12, 2026)

1. **Digital Census 2027 Self-Enumeration**: Over 5.72 lakh households used the self-enumeration portal (se.census.gov.in) in its first phase. The portal was positioned as a "convenient" digital option, but critics pointed out it excludes those without digital access—ironically, those most in need of being counted.

2. **Equinix Mumbai Data Centre Launch**: The fourth IBX data centre in Mumbai was inaugurated, with executives explicitly linking India's DPI (Aadhaar, UPI, ONDC) to the digital economy's $1 trillion target by 2027-28. Privacy advocates raised concerns about increased data concentration in corporate infrastructure.

3. **Mandatory E-Arrival Cards**: India made online e-arrival cards mandatory for foreign nationals and OCI holders. While presented as a "digital streamlining," it raised questions about mandatory data collection patterns extending to all interactions.

4. **Smart Meter Backlash**: The Ministry of Power finally clarified that prepaid smart meters are "optional, not mandatory" in Parliament, after months of protests and viral videos showing forced installations and disconnections.

5. **Health Data Integration**: The Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) continued pushing ABHA creation at healthcare facilities, with reports emerging that many hospitals make ABHA "registration" a precondition for treatment—effectively mandatory.

## ⚖️ Citizen Rights Advice

**What You Can Do:**

1. **For Aadhaar-linked services**: You cannot *currently* refuse Aadhaar for essential services, but you CAN:
   - Request service without Aadhaar in writing
   - File complaints on UIDAI's grievance portal
   - Keep copies of all refusal letters for future RTI queries

2. **For Digital Census**: If you cannot self-enumerate:
   - Wait for door-to-door enumeration (mandatory in-person option exists)
   - Do not share OTP or login credentials with "helpline" callers
   - Verify enumerator credentials before sharing any data

3. **For ABHA/Health Records**:
   - You can OPT OUT of ABHA linking at any time
   - Demand treatment WITHOUT ABHA creation—it's your legal right
   - Use the ABHA privacy settings to restrict data sharing

4. **For Smart Meters**:
   - Demand the "opt-out" form from your electricity provider
   - If already installed without consent, file complaint with consumer forum
   - Note: You CANNOT be disconnected for refusing a smart meter

5. **For ONDC**:
   - You're not required to use ONDC for any transaction
   - Compare prices across platforms before buying
   - Report misleading "free" offers to Consumer Affairs

**Know Your Rights:**
- The 2018 Supreme Court judgment explicitly states Aadhaar cannot be mandatory for services beyond welfare distribution
- Digital personal data is protected under DPDP Act 2023—demand data deletion when possible
- You can file RTI queries about your DPI data usage—no entity can refuse
