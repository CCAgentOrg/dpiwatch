# DPI Brief — July 09, 2026


## L2: RBI Reasserts Crypto Prohibition Stance; Digital Rupee Gets Parliamentary Review

The Reserve Bank of India has doubled down on its long-standing opposition to cryptocurrencies, telling a parliamentary panel that India's crypto policy should "lean towards prohibition" to keep virtual digital assets outside the regulated financial system. Government documents reviewed by [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/india/india-central-bank-backs-crypto-ban-tax-department-warns-evasion-risks-documents-2026-07-08/) reveal that the central bank's position remains firmly against legalising crypto as currency. Meanwhile, the Income Tax Department flagged that trading via offshore exchanges is hard to monitor, with estimates suggesting India has nearly 39 million crypto traders holding approximately $2.1 billion in digital assets as of May 2026.

Separately, the Parliamentary Standing Committee on Finance held its seventh round of discussions on the virtual digital assets ecosystem on July 2, with officials from RBI and ICAI in attendance. The committee reviewed progress on India's Central Bank Digital Currency — the digital rupee (e₹) — which has crossed 12 million users in its pilot phase. The panel also explored potential policy interventions to strengthen India's digital financial ecosystem while ensuring adequate safeguards. The RBI has additionally indicated interest in linking the digital rupee with CBDCs from other BRICS nations, a proposal that may gain traction ahead of the 2026 BRICS summit. ([Kalkine](https://www.kalkine.co.in/article/blockchain-crypto/rbi-pushes-digital-rupee-over-stablecoins-proposes-brics-currency-linkage))

## L2: $50 Billion IPO Pipeline at Risk — PhonePe, Jio Financial, NSE in the Crosshairs

India's blockbuster IPO pipeline worth approximately $50 billion is facing fresh uncertainty after escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. CNBC reported that the pipeline — which includes mega-listings from UPI giants PhonePe and Jio Financial Services, as well as the National Stock Exchange — was gathering momentum after a slow first half of 2026. However, the renewed US-Iran conflict has sent oil prices surging and roiled global markets, with India's Nifty 50 and BSE Sensex logging their worst single-day decline in over three months on July 8. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/09/india-ipo-50-billion-jio-phonepe-nse.html))

This is particularly significant for the DPI ecosystem: PhonePe's IPO would be the largest public market debut for a UPI payments entity, and Jio Financial's listing signals the deepening intersection of digital payments, fintech, and India's broader financial infrastructure. The RBI's measures to support the rupee — including steps to boost foreign inflows — helped pare back rate-hike expectations, with five-year OIS dropping to a four-month low of 6.1%.

## L4 & L6: Digital India Awareness Workshop in Ladakh; ONDC Crosses 200 Million Transactions

A Digital India State Awareness Workshop was held in Leh, Ladakh (UT), bringing together government stakeholders to showcase India's digital public infrastructure. The workshop featured presentations on ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce), which has now crossed 200 million cumulative transactions, with the last 100 million coming in just six months — a sign of accelerating adoption. Government e-Marketplace (GeM), which enables small sellers to supply goods and services across government arms, was also highlighted. ([Digital India / MeitY](https://www.facebook.com/meityindia/posts/the-digital-india-state-awareness-workshop-in-leh-ladakh-ut-brought-together-gov/1343065431342558))

Meanwhile, in Sikkim, a SEED (Sikkim Entrepreneurship and Economic Development) cell event on July 8 trained local entrepreneurs on accessing central government schemes, with sessions covering ONDC, GeM, and e-commerce platforms for expanded market access. These grassroots programmes underline the government's push to extend DPI access to underserved regions, aligning with the cooperative sector reform agenda that includes GeM/ONDC access and tax parity for cooperatives. ([UPSCPDF](https://upscpdf.com/2026/07/08/grand-ambitions-for-indias-cooperative-sector-reforming-cooperative-governance-for-inclusive-growth-upscpdf-editorial-analysis))

## L7: Quantum-Safe Infrastructure Gains Urgency as Cyber Threats Accelerate

The global cybersecurity landscape is intensifying pressure on digital infrastructure worldwide, with implications for India's DPI stack. The CrowdStrike Global Threat Report 2026 notes that average attacker breakout times have fallen to just 29 minutes, meaning adversaries can move from initial network entry to data exfiltration in under half an hour. With AI being weaponised on both offence and defence, the pace of attacks is expected to accelerate further. ([Infosecurity Magazine](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news-features/faster-cyberattacks-reshape/))

Separately, a Newsweek analysis argues that quantum computing will eventually force banks, digital platforms, and governments to upgrade to quantum-safe encryption — and that this transition must be compatible with existing infrastructure rather than requiring wholesale rebuilds. For India, this is particularly relevant to Aadhaar (L1), UPI (L2), and DigiLocker (L3), all of which handle sensitive personal and financial data at massive scale. CERT-In's proactive posture on emerging threats will need to incorporate quantum-readiness planning as the technology matures. ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/quantum-readiness-starts-with-the-infrastructure-we-already-have-12171614))
