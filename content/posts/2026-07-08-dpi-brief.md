# DPI Brief — July 08, 2026


## 1. UPI Expands to Greece, Now Live in 10 Countries [L2 — Payments]

India's Unified Payments Interface has officially launched in **Greece**, marking its expansion to **10 countries** globally. The rollout, enabled through a partnership between **Eurobank** and **NPCI International**, allows users of UPI-enabled applications to make digital payments at merchants across Greece. UPI's international footprint now spans Bhutan, Nepal, UAE, Singapore, Sri Lanka, Mauritius, France, Peru, Oman, and Greece — a remarkable trajectory from a domestic rail to a global payments standard. [UPI in Greece](https://thepaypers.com/payments/news/upi-launches-in-greece-expands-to-10-countries)

Separately, NPCI has partnered with **HSBC India** and **JPMorgan Payments** to enable **real-time FX settlement for international UPI payments**, a critical step toward reducing friction in cross-border remittances and making UPI a viable rail for outbound payments beyond India's borders.

## 2. NPCI Deploys AI for Fraud Detection in Digital Payments [L2 — Payments]

The National Payments Corporation of India has launched a **pilot programme using Artificial Intelligence** to track fraudulent transactions across the UPI network. The AI-driven system is designed to identify suspicious patterns — including mule account networks and coordinated fraud — in real time, addressing the growing sophistication of digital payment scams. This aligns with broader regulatory concerns flagged in the RBI's latest Financial Stability Report. [Economic Times](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/economy/finance/npci-turns-to-ai-to-stop-frauds-from-siphoning-off-money/articleshow/132078821.cms)

NPCI is also developing a **new UPI AutoPay mandate management feature** that would let users view all their recurring UPI mandates from any single UPI app, improving transparency and control over automatic deductions.

## 3. RBI Flags AI-Enabled Cyberattacks as Top Near-Term Threat to Financial System [L7 — Trust]

The Reserve Bank of India's latest **Financial Stability Report (June 2026)** has classified **AI-enabled cyberattacks as the "most important" near-term cyber threat** to India's banking and financial system. The RBI survey of financial institutions revealed that while banks have established robust practices in vulnerability assessment and penetration testing, **cybersecurity awareness and training for employees** need significant strengthening. The report also noted that India's banking sector remains resilient, with gross NPAs touching a **multi-decadal low of 1.8%** as of March 2026. [Telegraph India](https://www.telegraphindia.com/business/rbi-financial-stability-report-flags-ai-cyber-risks-as-bank-npas-hit-1-8-per-cent-prnt/cid/2167984)

## 4. Digital India Completes 11 Years — MeitY Showcases DPI Stack [L1/L3/L6 — Cross-Layer]

The Digital India programme marked its **11th anniversary on July 1, 2026**, with MeitY and Digital India Corporation highlighting the scale of India's DPI ecosystem. Key milestones showcased include:

- **BHASHINI** — 36+ languages supported, 1.2 million mobile downloads, impacting over 100 crore Indians through voice-first multilingual solutions
- **API Setu** — A unified platform managing the entire API lifecycle, enabling seamless data exchange across government platforms
- **UMANG** — 11.6+ crore registered users with over **793 crore transactions** processed
- **India Stack Global** — Now showcasing 15 key DPI building blocks (Aadhaar, UPI, U-WIN, API Setu, DigiLocker, CoWIN, GeM, UMANG, DIKSHA, eSanjeevani, eHospital, eOffice, eCourts, POSHAN Tracker, NCD Platform) in all UN languages

The 11-year milestone underscores how India has built one of the world's largest DPI ecosystems at population scale, with UPI transaction volumes growing **557x between 2017 and 2026**. ## 5. UPI Statistics — May 2026: 23.2 Billion Transactions, ₹29.9 Lakh Crore [L2 — Payments]

NPCI's latest published statistics for **May 2026** show UPI processing **23,201.93 million transactions** valued at **₹29,90,424.21 crore** (~₹29.9 lakh crore), with **720 banks** now live on the network. This represents continued month-on-month growth from April 2026 (22,346.80 million transactions, ₹29,02,988.05 crore, 713 banks). India now accounts for approximately **50% of the world's real-time digital transactions**, driven by 49 crore+ UPI users. [NPCI Statistics](https://www.npci.org.in/what-we-do/upi/product-statistics)
