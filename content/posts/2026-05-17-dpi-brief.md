# DPI Brief — May 17, 2026

## UPI Transaction Milestone: 22.35 Billion in April 2026

India's Unified Payments Interface (UPI) continues to demonstrate remarkable growth, recording **22.35 billion transactions** in April 2026[^1]. This milestone underscores UPI's position as the backbone of India's retail payments ecosystem, with the system now handling **over 600 million transactions daily**[^2]. The payment infrastructure has become a symbol of India's technological prowess, with the Prime Minister highlighting India's low-cost, open digital infrastructure for 1.4 billion people at the AI Action Summit[^3].

## ONDC Reaches 12 Million Orders in July

The Open Network for Digital Commerce (ONDC) achieved a significant milestone by processing **12 million orders in July**[^4]. Of these, 4.4 million came from the mobility sector, while 7.6 million were from non-mobility domains including food, groceries, and fashion. The food and beverages sector led with 1.7 million transactions, while groceries and fashion contributed 1.4 million and 0.6 million respectively[^4]. An independent study by ONDC network participants revealed the platform has captured an **18% market share in food and beverages within Bengaluru** and a **3% national share**[^4].

## India Post Joins ONDC as Logistics Partner

In a landmark development for India's digital commerce ecosystem, the **Department of Posts successfully delivered its first ONDC order** as a Logistics Service Provider[^5]. Leveraging its extensive nationwide postal network, India Post will now support seamless and cost-effective logistics for e-commerce transactions on the ONDC platform. This milestone underscores India Post's expanding role in the digital economy and its transition into a technology-enabled logistics partner[^5].

## FICCI & ONDC Sign MoU for Travel, Tourism & Hospitality

The Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI) and ONDC have signed a **Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)** to enhance digital experiences within the travel, tourism, and hospitality sectors[^6]. This collaboration aims to integrate ONDC's open network protocols with FICCI's extensive industry connections, potentially transforming how travel services are delivered and consumed across India.

## Cyber Crime Crackdown: ₹1.59 Crore Refunded to Digital Arrest Victims

The Cyberabad Police Commissionerate conducted a major crackdown on "Digital Arrest" scams between May 6-14, 2026[^7]. The operation resulted in **10 arrests** and the recovery of ₹1.59 Crore refunded to victims across 64 cases[^7]. The crackdown targeted sophisticated fraud networks that used intimidation tactics to trick victims into transferring funds, highlighting the growing challenge of digital fraud in India's expanding digital economy.

## India-Morocco DPI Cooperation

India and Morocco are strengthening cooperation through **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI)**, with a focus on digital identity and education[^8]. This partnership represents India's growing role in exporting its DPI expertise globally, demonstrating how homegrown digital infrastructure can serve as a model for other developing nations seeking to leapfrog traditional digital transformation pathways.
