# DPI Brief — May 02, 2026

## Today's DPI Updates (7 Layers)

### L1: Identity & Authentication
**No major updates today.** UIDAI's official channels show routine operations — Aadhaar updates continue to process within 90 days as standard. eKYC services remain operational via [myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in](https://myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in).

### L2: Payments & Financial Rails

**Major UPI market concentration hearing scheduled.** Executives from Amazon Pay, WhatsApp (Meta), CRED, MobiKwik, and Flipkart's Super.money met with NPCI this week to raise concerns over PhonePe and Google Pay's dominant market position. The two players accounted for **~80% of 22.6 billion UPI transactions in March 2026**, effectively blocking smaller players from scaling. This follows years of deliberation on implementing NPCI's 2022 recommendation to cap individual app market share at 30% — a cap that was delayed, allowing the duopoly to entrench further. [^1]

**RBI cancels Paytm Payments Bank licence.** The Reserve Bank of India officially cancelled the banking licence of Paytm Payments Bank (PPBL) in a final regulatory action. The parent company One97 Communications (Paytm) stated it has no exposure to PPBL. The bank had been under RBI restrictions since 2022 for persistent compliance failures. [^2]

### L3: Documents & Data Exchange

**DigiLocker emerges as national academic repository.** Multiple state education boards are directing students to download board results via DigiLocker — Assam HS, Karnataka SSLC, and UP Board results all referenced DigiLocker notices for scorecard distribution. This marks a quiet but significant shift in how academic credentials are being disseminated, with DigiLocker positioning itself as the authoritative digital vault for Class 10 and 12 results across states. [^3]

### L4: Commerce & Logistics

**GeM continues volume growth.** The Government e-Marketplace processed significant procurement volume in Q1 FY27, with the platform seeing expanded adoption across central ministries and state governments. No major policy changes reported this cycle.

### L5: Sectoral Infrastructure

**Census 2027 self-enumeration begins in Punjab.** The Digital India portal launched self-enumeration for Census 2027 in Punjab this week. The digital census uses DPI infrastructure for identity verification and will progressively roll out across other states. This is a key test of using Aadhaar-linked digital identity for large-scale government data collection. [^4]

**Axis Bank tech investments cut 3,000 jobs.** Axis Bank reported a reduction of 3,000 roles in FY26 (headcount fell to 1.01 lakh) while adding 400 branches. CEO Amitabh Chaudhry attributed the efficiency gains to long-term digital investments — a pattern increasingly cited as banks automate KYC, credit processing, and customer service via Aadhaar-linked and UPI-adjacent systems. [^5]

### L6: Governance & Grievance

**No major CPGRAMS/ DARPG updates today.** The grievances portal continues normal operations. Last notable policy development was the expansion of CPGRAMS integration with state government systems in Q4 FY26.

### L7: Security, Privacy & Trust

**Linux kernel zero-day "CopyFail" disclosed.** A critical privilege-escalation vulnerability (CVE, publicly disclosed April 29, 2026) affects **all major Linux distributions shipped since 2017**. The flaw allows any unprivileged local user to obtain root access by exploiting a controlled 4-byte write into the kernel's page cache via `AF_ALG` sockets. Patches were committed to mainline on April 1; users should patch immediately. [^6]

**Global cybersecurity agencies warn on Agentic AI risks.** CISA (US), NCSC (UK), ASD (Australia), and five other agencies jointly released guidance warning that AI agents — systems that autonomously plan and execute tasks — introduce significant attack surface expansion. Every component in an agentic AI pipeline (models, tools, memory, orchestration layers) creates new exploitation vectors. [^7]

**Windows SmartScreen bypass actively exploited.** A zero-click vulnerability (CVE-2026-32202) stemming from an incomplete patch for a Fancy Bear (APT28) exploit chain is being actively weaponized. Microsoft addressed the initial flaw in February 2026 but a regression created a new hole allowing NTLM hash theft simply by opening a folder containing a malicious `.lnk` file. [^8]
