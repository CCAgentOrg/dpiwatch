# DPI Brief — July 14, 2026

## Truecaller vs TRAI: Anti-Spam Framework Clashes with Caller ID Ecosystem (L7 – Trust/TRAI)

Truecaller's CEO Rishit Jhunjhunwala publicly challenged the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) over its directive preventing caller identification apps from labelling calls from the designated 1400 (telemarketing) and 1600 (transactional/service) number series as spam. TRAI has reportedly sought powers under the Information Technology Act to take action against caller ID platforms like Truecaller, Hiya, and Whoscall for flagging these numbers. Truecaller, which counts India as its largest market with 273 million users, argues that the restriction has enabled abuse of those number series and eroded trust in legitimate business calls. The company also questioned whether TRAI has jurisdiction over internet-based call management platforms, suggesting oversight should rest with MeitY instead. The standoff pits India's anti-spam regulatory framework against the consumer demand for scam detection tools — a tension with no clean resolution in sight. [^1] [^2]

## DPI Conclave: NPCI Signals Next Decade of UPI Focuses on AI Fraud Detection and Cross-Border Expansion (L2 – Payments)

The Chintan Research Foundation, in partnership with Ikigai Law and Koan Advisory Group, convened a high-level conclave on digital payments in New Delhi, bringing together policymakers, regulators, and industry leaders. Dr. Balakrishnan Mahadevan, former COO of NPCI, outlined the next phase of UPI's evolution: moving from the current decade's focus on interoperability and scale to the next decade's priorities of resilience, redundancy, and AI-enabled fraud detection. Former Finance Secretary Ashok Lavasa emphasised that integrated infrastructure, harmonised standards, and clear jurisdictional frameworks are critical for the internationalisation of India's payment systems. The conclave also addressed the evolving role of cryptocurrencies, stablecoins, and the digital rupee, with the Financial Intelligence Unit–India confirming it oversees virtual asset service providers from an AML/CFT perspective under India's technologically-neutral, risk-based regulatory approach. [^3]

## TRAI Hosts Responsible AI in Telecom Session at India AI Impact Summit 2026 (L7 – Trust/TRAI)

TRAI hosted a session on "Responsible AI in Telecom" at the India AI Impact Summit 2026, focusing on digital resilience, governance, and network transformation. The session highlighted the intersection of AI deployment in telecommunications infrastructure with the need for robust governance frameworks. As India's telecom networks increasingly integrate AI for network optimisation, customer service, and fraud detection, TRAI's engagement signals that regulatory thinking around AI in critical infrastructure is maturing — moving from theoretical frameworks to operational considerations of how AI governance will coexist with telecom regulation in practice. [^4]

## RBI Digital Payment Fraud Safeguards Spark Policy Debate (L2 – Payments)

The Reserve Bank of India's recent discussion paper titled *Exploring Safeguards in Digital Payments to Curb Frauds* has generated significant policy debate. The paper proposes introducing "positive frictions" in the payments system to combat rising fraud, including a one-hour lag on Aadhaar-to-Payment Address (APP) transactions above ₹10,000 — a threshold that covers 45% of reported fraud cases and approximately 98.5% of total value lost. While commentators acknowledge the RBI is right to act, some safeguards need sharper design. The discussion paper reflects growing concern that India's zero-fee, frictionless UPI architecture, while world-leading in scale, creates an outsized attack surface for increasingly sophisticated financial fraud. NPCI has separately initiated a pilot for an AI model to detect and track stolen money in real-time as it moves across bank accounts, aiming to intercept fraudulent transactions before funds vanish. [^5] [^6]

## Google Cloud Deploys Gemini AI Models on Indian Infrastructure (L7 – Trust/MeitY)

Google Cloud CEO Thomas Kurian announced that the company is now deploying its latest Gemini AI models on infrastructure located inside India, allowing enterprises to keep both data and AI processing within the country. The move is driven by growing demand for digital sovereignty and marks India's emergence as a strategic hub in Google's global AI strategy. This development is directly relevant to India's DPI ecosystem — as platforms like DigiLocker, ONDC, and ABDM increasingly rely on cloud-based AI services, the availability of sovereign AI infrastructure reduces data residency concerns that have been a persistent friction point in DPI adoption. [^7]
