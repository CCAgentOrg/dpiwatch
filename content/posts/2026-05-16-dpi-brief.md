# DPI Brief — May 16, 2026

## Aadhaar Identity Layer (L1): UIDAI Expands Digital Identity Services

**UIDAI Extends Free Document Update Facility**  
UIDAI has extended the free Aadhaar document update deadline, now operational from June 15, 2026 through June 14, 2027[^1]. This allows citizens to update demographic and biometric details without paying the standard fees of ₹50 for demographic updates and ₹100 for biometric updates[^2].

**New Aadhaar App Replaces mAadhaar**  
UIDAI is retiring the legacy mAadhaar app and replacing it with a new Aadhaar app featuring enhanced security features including QR-based sharing, offline verification capabilities, and improved biometric controls[^3]. Users are being urged to migrate their data to the new application before the old app is decommissioned[^4].

**UIDAI CEO Highlights Aadhaar's Role in Good Governance**  
UIDAI CEO Shri Vivek Chandra Verma delivered a keynote address at the 'AI Transformation Conclave', emphasizing the growing usage and utility of Aadhaar as a tool of good governance[^5]. He underscored various features of the ecosystem that strengthen digital identity infrastructure[^6].

**Offline Verification Expansion**  
UIDAI awarded OVSE certificates in Lucknow to seven firms promoting Aadhaar offline verification[^7]. This initiative enables secure, privacy-friendly ID checks without requiring internet connectivity, expanding access to digital identity services in offline environments[^8].

## Governance Layer (L6): Census 2027 Digital Self-Enumeration

**Census 2027 Self-Enumeration Phase Concludes in Nagpur**  
The self-enumeration phase of India's 16th National Census concluded in Nagpur on May 15, 2026, with citizens being asked to complete online registration before field surveys begin[^9]. The digital registration process, which began on May 1, provides a final opportunity for residents to submit their details online[^10].

**Online Census Portal**  
Citizens can complete the registration process by visiting [se.census.gov.in](https://se.census.gov.in)[^11]. After filling out the online form, the head of the family receives an 11-digit SE ID[^12]. Field operations are scheduled to begin from May 16 and continue through June 14, 2026[^13].

**Data Collection Scope**  
The census will record data related to family members, head of family, social category, and facilities including drinking water, electricity, sanitation, and LPG connections[^14].

## Sectoral Layer (L5): Digital Public Infrastructure for Cultural Heritage

**Madhya Pradesh Tribal Art Platform**  
Madhya Pradesh, home to the largest tribal population in India, showcased Gond and Baiga tribal art through the Aadirang platform[^15]. This dedicated e-commerce and digital empowerment platform, launched in March 2021 by the Madhya Pradesh Tribal Affairs Department, enables tribal artists from forests in Dindori, Mandla, Shahdol, and adjoining regions to reach collectors across India and abroad without exploitative middlemen[^16].

**Cultural Heritage as Economic Asset**  
The Madhya Pradesh Tourism Board views tribal art not merely as cultural heritage but as a living economic and creative force integral to India's identity[^17]. Institutions such as the Tribal Museum in Bhopal continue to provide immersive cultural context to these traditions[^18].

## Key DPI Developments

**Labour Code Reforms for Migrant Workers**  
New labour code rules notified on May 8, 2026, provide significant protections for inter-state migrant workers[^19]. The OSH&WC Code, 2020, which now operationalizes these rules, fundamentally rewrites the definition of an inter-state migrant worker under the new framework[^20].
