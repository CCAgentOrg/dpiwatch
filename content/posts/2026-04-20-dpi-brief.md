# DPI Brief — April 20, 2026

## Today's DPI Updates (7 Layers)

### L1: Identity & Authentication

**Aadhaar Pre-Installation Mandate Dropped After Industry Pushback**

In a significant policy reversal, the Indian government has decided not to mandate pre-installation of the Aadhaar authentication app on smartphones. UIDAI had proposed in January 2026 that Apple, Samsung, Google, and other major smartphone manufacturers pre-install the Aadhaar app on devices sold in India. Following pushback from the smartphone industry, MeitY reviewed the proposal and UIDAI stated it is "not in favour of mandating the pre-installation of the Aadhaar App on smartphones." The move signals continued tension between identity verification ambitions and device ecosystem concerns. [^1]

### L2: Payments & Financial Rails

No major UPI/NPCI announcements today. For the latest transaction volumes, refer to [NPCI's official statistics](https://www.npci.org.in/what-we-do/upi/product-statistics).

### L3: Documents & Data Exchange

**CBSE Class 10 Results 2026 Live on DigiLocker**

The Central Board of Secondary Education declared Class 10 Board Exam Results 2026 on April 15, with results made immediately available on DigiLocker alongside UMANG app and official CBSE portals (cbseresults.nic.in, results.cbse.nic.in). This continues the trend of exam results being digitally issued and accessible via government DPI infrastructure, reducing dependence on physical mark sheets and enabling faster verification through the documents layer. [^2]

### L4: Commerce & Logistics

**India E-Retail GMV Reached $65-66 Billion in 2025**

Bain & Company's latest report indicates India's e-retail market crossed $65-66 billion in GMV during 2025, growing at a 19-21% CAGR. The sector is shifting toward instant delivery models, Tier 2+ city expansion, and early-stage AI-led shopping experiences. Bain projects e-retail advertising now accounts for ~25% of digital ad spend, with GMV expected to reach $170-180 billion by 2030 at 20%+ CAGR. ONDC continues to be a key DPI layer in this expansion, particularly for open network adoption in smaller cities. [^3]

### L5: Sectoral Infrastructure

**Union Budget 2026-27: Crop-Specific Agri Strategy & Census 2027 Self-Enumeration Goes Live**

The Union Budget 2026-27 announced a strategic shift toward crop-specific and regionally aligned agricultural strategies. Key highlights: India's agricultural sector recorded 4.45% record growth over the last decade; the country ranks second globally in coconut production (supporting 30 million livelihoods); agarwood plantation sector expanding with 150 million trees as of January 2026, 90% in North-Eastern states. Meanwhile, Census 2027 self-enumeration for house listing began April 16 in Haryana (including Gurgaon, Faridabad), Chandigarh, Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Arunachal Pradesh, and Chhattisgarh, with citizens encouraged to use the online se.census.gov.in portal. [^4]

### L6: Governance & Grievance

No major CPGRAMS or eOffice announcements today. Refer to [DARPG official updates](https://darpg.gov.in) and the [CPGRAMS portal](https://pgportal.gov.in) for the latest grievance redressal statistics.

### L7: Security, Privacy & Trust

**Microsoft April 2026 Patch Tuesday: 167 Vulnerabilities, 2 Zero-Days**

Microsoft's April 2026 Patch Tuesday addressed 167 security flaws including two actively exploited zero-days. Notably, three Microsoft Defender zero-day vulnerabilities (codenamed BlueHawk, RedSun, and UnDefend) were disclosed by researcher Chaotic Eclipse after reportedly unsatisfactory handling of the disclosure process by Microsoft. Two of these remain unpatched at time of writing. Meanwhile, CISA added six known exploited vulnerabilities to its catalog affecting Fortinet FortiClient EMS (CVE-2026-21643, SQL injection, CVSS 9.1), Microsoft Windows, Exchange Server, and Visual Basic for Applications. [^5]

**Global Cybersecurity Landscape: AI-Accelerated Threats**

IBM announced new AI-powered cybersecurity measures including an Autonomous Security service with AI agents for automated vulnerability remediation at machine speed. OpenAI launched GPT-5.4-Cyber, a variant optimized for defensive cybersecurity use cases, expanding its Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) program. Check Point introduced Secure AI Advisory Service and Check Point Exposure Management for AI-era threats. [^6]
