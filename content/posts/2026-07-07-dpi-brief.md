# DPI Brief — July 07, 2026


## India Widens Platform Accountability Crackdown: Notices to WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal Over Anonymous Messaging

**Layer: L7 — Trust (CERT-In, MeitY, IT Rules)**

India's Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) has dramatically expanded its regulatory sweep over encrypted messaging platforms, issuing notices to Telegram and Signal demanding explanations for their phone-number concealment features. This follows MeitY's earlier action ordering WhatsApp — India's largest messaging platform with over 500 million users — to halt its planned username feature rollout within three days or face regulatory action. The notices come on the heels of India's temporary blockade of Telegram last month over leaks of national medical exam papers. The Internet Freedom Foundation has criticised the government's actions, arguing that the notice to Signal in particular threatens protected speech given its widespread use by journalists and activists. For India's DPI trust layer, this marks an aggressive interpretation of the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021, signalling that platform-level anonymity features are now squarely in MeitY's crosshairs. The regulatory posture raises important questions about the balance between traceability mandates and end-to-end encryption guarantees.

## MeitY Orders Meta to Remove Child Abuse Ads on Instagram; Second Warning in a Week

**Layer: L7 — Trust (CERT-In, MeitY, IT Rules)**

In a separate but related enforcement action, MeitY has directed Meta to immediately disable advertisements on Instagram that promote or facilitate child sexual abuse material (CSAM), following a BBC Eye investigation that found Instagram running paid adverts promoting such content targeted at Indian users. The government has sought an explanation from Meta within one week on how paid advertisements containing CSAM were permitted on the platform. Meta stated it has a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse material and is strengthening detection systems. This is the second warning to Meta within a single week — the first being the WhatsApp username crackdown — underscoring the mounting regulatory pressure on the social media giant in its largest market by user base. For India's trust infrastructure, these actions demonstrate that MeitY is increasingly willing to use its intermediary rules powers proactively against major platforms, rather than relying solely on post-facto compliance.

## Apple Restores Card Payments in India After Four-Year Hiatus, Adapting to RBI Framework

**Layer: L2 — Payments (UPI, NPCI, RuPay)**

Apple has begun restoring bank card payments for Apple Account purchases in India, more than four years after suspending the option in May 2022. The phased rollout allows Indian users to link eligible Visa and Mastercard credit and debit cards to their Apple Account for App Store purchases, iCloud+ subscriptions, and Apple Music. The original withdrawal was triggered by the Reserve Bank of India's 2021 mandate requiring merchants and payment providers to implement strong customer authentication and use tokenized card data for recurring transactions. Apple's return signals that it has now built the necessary compliance infrastructure around RBI's card tokenisation and Additional Factor of Authentication (AFA) requirements. While Apple's services revenue in India has continued to grow at double-digit rates through UPI and other channels, restoring card payments becomes critical as the installed base expands and users demand more payment flexibility. The development is notable for India's payments layer: it demonstrates that global platforms will ultimately adapt to India's regulatory framework rather than abandon the market, reinforcing the policy leverage that RBI has built through its payments rules.

## NITI Aayog Recommends 90-Day Visa-on-Arrival and e-Visa Expansion to 175 Countries

**Layer: L3 — Documents (DigiLocker, APISetu, e-Visa)** | **Layer: L1 — Identity (Aadhaar)**

A new report by NITI Aayog in partnership with the Ministry of Tourism recommends introducing a 90-day, multiple-entry Tourist Visa-on-Arrival (VoA) scheme for eligible countries, a significant expansion from the current limited VoA facility available only to citizens of South Korea, Japan, and the UAE. The report also documents progress on India's e-Visa system, which between August 2025 and February 2026 has expanded eligibility to 175 countries, introduced new e-Visa categories, and added facilitation at more airports, seaports, and land checkpoints. The recommendations are part of a broader effort to modernise India's visa and immigration infrastructure — critical for a country positioning itself as a top tourism destination. For the DPI ecosystem, the e-Visa expansion represents a tangible application of India's digital document and identity infrastructure at scale, processing millions of applications through online systems. The proposed VoA upgrade would further digitise the entry process, reducing physical documentation and queuing at borders.

## PM Modi Embarks on Indo-Pacific Tour; Digital Partnerships Expected

**Layer: L6 — Governance (CPGRAMS, eOffice)** | Cross-cutting

Prime Minister Narendra Modi departed on a six-day visit to Indonesia, Australia, and New Zealand on July 6, with digital cooperation expected to feature prominently in bilateral discussions. India and Australia have been deepening digital economy ties, including cooperation on digital public infrastructure sharing — Australia has expressed interest in learning from India's DPI model for its own digital identity and payments initiatives. Indonesia, as ASEAN's largest economy, represents a key market for India's cross-border UPI expansion. The third India-Australia Annual Summit in Melbourne on July 8-10 is expected to yield new agreements spanning digital trade, fintech interoperability, and cybersecurity cooperation. The India-U.S. trade deal deadline of July 9 adds another layer of significance, with digital services and data governance rules likely on the agenda.
